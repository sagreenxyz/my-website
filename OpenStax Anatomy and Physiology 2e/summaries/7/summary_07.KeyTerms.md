# Summary: Chapter 7 Key Terms

This section lists important terminology related to the axial skeleton discussed in Chapter 7.

*(Note: The specific terms are derived from the HTML content. Below is a representation of the types of terms typically found.)*

*   **Axial Skeleton:** Central core of the skeleton (skull, vertebral column, thoracic cage).
*   **Cranial Bones:** Bones forming the braincase (frontal, parietal, temporal, occipital, sphenoid, ethmoid).
*   **Facial Bones:** Bones forming the face (maxilla, mandible, zygomatic, nasal, etc.).
*   **Fontanelle:** Soft spot in the infant skull where sutures haven't fully formed.
*   **Foramen Magnum:** Large opening in the occipital bone for the spinal cord.
*   **Hyoid Bone:** U-shaped bone in the neck, supporting the tongue.
*   **Intervertebral Disc:** Fibrocartilaginous pad between vertebrae.
*   **Kyphosis:** Excessive posterior curvature of the thoracic spine (humpback).
*   **Lordosis:** Excessive anterior curvature of the lumbar spine (swayback).
*   **Mandible:** Lower jaw bone.
*   **Maxilla:** Upper jaw bone.
*   **Occipital Condyle:** Projections on the occipital bone articulating with the atlas.
*   **Orbit:** Bony socket containing the eyeball.
*   **Paranasal Sinus:** Air-filled cavity within certain skull bones.
*   **Ribs:** Bones forming the lateral walls of the thoracic cage (true, false, floating).
*   **Sacrum:** Bone formed by the fusion of five sacral vertebrae.
*   **Scoliosis:** Abnormal lateral curvature of the spine.
*   **Sella Turcica:** Depression in the sphenoid bone housing the pituitary gland.
*   **Skull:** Bony structure of the head, including cranial and facial bones.
*   **Sternum:** Breastbone (manubrium, body, xiphoid process).
*   **Suture:** Immovable fibrous joint between skull bones.
*   **Thoracic Cage:** Ribs, sternum, and thoracic vertebrae.
*   **Vertebra:** Individual bone of the spinal column (cervical, thoracic, lumbar).
*   **Vertebral Column:** Spine, composed of vertebrae, sacrum, and coccyx.
*   **Zygomatic Arch:** Cheekbone structure formed by zygomatic and temporal bones.
