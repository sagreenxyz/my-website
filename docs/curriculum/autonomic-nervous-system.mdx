# The Autonomic Nervous System (ANS): Involuntary Control of Visceral Functions

**Module 1: Introduction to the Autonomic Nervous System**

Defining the ANS:
* What is the autonomic nervous system (ANS)?
* What types of effector tissues does the ANS primarily control?
* Is the control exerted by the ANS generally voluntary or involuntary?

Functions of the ANS:
* Describe the overall role of the ANS in maintaining homeostasis.
* List the major physiological processes regulated by the ANS (e.g., heart rate, blood pressure, digestion, respiration, glandular secretions).

Divisions of the ANS:
* What are the two main functional divisions of the ANS?

**Module 2: Sympathetic Division - The "Fight-or-Flight" System**

Overview of the Sympathetic Division:
* When is the sympathetic division typically most active?
* What are the general physiological responses associated with sympathetic activation?

Anatomical Features:
* Describe the origin of preganglionic sympathetic neurons in the spinal cord.
* Explain the location of sympathetic ganglia (sympathetic trunk ganglia and prevertebral ganglia).
* Describe the pathways of preganglionic and postganglionic sympathetic fibers.

Neurotransmitters and Receptors:
* What are the primary neurotransmitters released by preganglionic and postganglionic sympathetic neurons?
* List the main types of adrenergic receptors (alpha and beta) and their general effects.

**Module 3: Parasympathetic Division - The "Rest-and-Digest" System**

Overview of the Parasympathetic Division:

* What are the general physiological responses associated with parasympathetic activation?

Anatomical Features:
* Describe the origin of preganglionic parasympathetic neurons in the brainstem and sacral spinal cord.

Neurotransmitters and Receptors:
* What is the primary neurotransmitter released by preganglionic and postganglionic parasympathetic neurons?
* List the main types of cholinergic receptors (nicotinic and muscarinic) and their general effects.
