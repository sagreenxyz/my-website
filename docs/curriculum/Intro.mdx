# Introduction to Human Anatomy and Physiology

**Module 1: Foundational Concepts**

Defining Anatomy and Physiology:
* What is anatomy, and what are its main branches (e.g., gross anatomy, microscopic anatomy)?
* What is physiology, and how does it relate to anatomy?
* Why is it important to study anatomy and physiology together?

Levels of Structural Organization:
* List and describe the different levels of structural organization in the human body, from the simplest to the most complex.
* Provide examples of structures found at each level (e.g., atoms, molecules, cells, tissues, organs, organ systems, organism).

Basic Life Processes:
* What are the fundamental characteristics or processes that distinguish living organisms?
* Provide a brief explanation of each of these life processes (e.g., metabolism, responsiveness, movement, growth, differentiation, reproduction).

Homeostasis: Maintaining Internal Balance:
* Define homeostasis and explain its importance for the survival of an organism.
* Describe the components of a feedback loop (receptor, control center, effector).
* Compare and contrast negative and positive feedback mechanisms. Provide a physiological example of each.

Anatomical Terminology: Directional Terms:
* Explain the meaning of the following directional terms and provide an example of their use: superior/inferior, anterior/posterior, medial/lateral, proximal/distal, superficial/deep.

Anatomical Terminology: Body Regions and Planes:
* Identify and describe the major regions of the human body (e.g., cephalic, cervical, thoracic, abdominal, pelvic, upper limb, lower limb).
* Describe the three main anatomical planes (sagittal, frontal/coronal, transverse/horizontal) and how they divide the body.

**Module 2: Basic Chemistry and Cell Biology**

Basic Chemistry for Biology:
* What are the main types of chemical bonds relevant to human physiology (e.g., ionic, covalent, hydrogen)?
* Why is water essential for life? Describe its key properties.
* Explain the concepts of acids, bases, and pH. What is the physiological pH range in the human body?

Organic Molecules: Building Blocks of Life:
* List the four main types of organic molecules found in the human body.
* For each type (carbohydrates, lipids, proteins, nucleic acids), describe their basic structure and major functions.

Cellular Structure: The Basic Unit of Life:
* Draw and label the major components of a typical human cell (e.g., plasma membrane, cytoplasm, nucleus, organelles).
* Describe the structure and primary functions of the plasma membrane.

Cellular Organelles and Their Functions:
* Describe the structure and function of the following organelles: nucleus, ribosomes, endoplasmic reticulum (rough and smooth), Golgi apparatus, mitochondria, lysosomes, peroxisomes.

Cellular Transport Mechanisms:
* Compare and contrast passive transport (e.g., diffusion, osmosis, facilitated diffusion) and active transport. Provide examples of each.
* Describe the processes of endocytosis and exocytosis.

**Module 3: Tissues of the Body**

Epithelial Tissue: Covering and Lining:
* What are the general characteristics and functions of epithelial tissue?
* Classify the different types of covering and lining epithelium based on cell shape and number of layers. Provide examples of where each type is found.
* Describe the structure and function of glandular epithelium. Distinguish between exocrine and endocrine glands.

Connective Tissue: Support and Connection:
* What are the general characteristics and functions of connective tissue?
* List the major types of connective tissue and their subtypes (e.g., loose connective tissue, dense connective tissue, cartilage, bone, blood).
* Describe the main components of connective tissue (cells, ground substance, fibers).

Muscle Tissue: Movement:
* What are the general characteristics and functions of muscle tissue?
* Compare and contrast the three types of muscle tissue (skeletal, cardiac, smooth) in terms of structure, location, and control.

Nervous Tissue: Communication and Control:
* What are the general characteristics and functions of nervous tissue?
* Describe the structure and function of neurons (nerve cells) and neuroglia (supporting cells).

Tissue Repair and Membranes:
* Describe the general process of tissue repair.
* List the four main types of membranes in the body (mucous, serous, cutaneous, synovial) and describe their structure and function.

**Module 4: The Integumentary System**

Structure of the Skin:
* Describe the two main layers of the skin (epidermis and dermis) and their major components.
* List the layers of the epidermis and describe the characteristics of each.
* What are the accessory structures of the skin?

Functions of the Integumentary System:
* Discuss the various functions of the skin, including protection, thermoregulation, sensation, vitamin D synthesis, and excretion.

Clinical Relevance: Common Skin Pathologies:
* Briefly describe the causes, symptoms, and basic treatment approaches for common skin conditions such as:
    * Infections (e.g., cellulitis, fungal infections)
    * Inflammatory conditions (e.g., eczema, psoriasis)
    * Skin cancers (basal cell carcinoma, squamous cell carcinoma, melanoma)
* What are some common pharmacological therapies used to treat these conditions (e.g., antibiotics, antifungals, corticosteroids, chemotherapy)?

**Module 5: The Skeletal System**

Bone Tissue and Structure:
* Describe the microscopic structure of bone tissue, including osteocytes, osteoblasts, and osteoclasts.
* Compare and contrast compact bone and spongy bone.
* Describe the gross structure of a long bone.

Functions of the Skeletal System:
* List and explain the major functions of the skeletal system (e.g., support, protection, movement, mineral storage, blood cell formation).

Joints (Articulations):
* Define a joint and classify joints based on their structure (fibrous, cartilaginous, synovial) and function (synarthrosis, amphiarthrosis, diarthrosis).
* Describe the general structure of a synovial joint.

Clinical Relevance: Skeletal Pathologies and Therapies:
* Briefly describe the causes, symptoms, and basic treatment approaches for common skeletal disorders such as:
    * Fractures (different types)
    * Osteoporosis
    * Osteoarthritis
* What are some common clinical therapies for these conditions (e.g., casting, surgery, physical therapy)?
* What are some common pharmacological therapies used (e.g., pain relievers, bisphosphonates)?

**Module 6: The Muscular System**

Skeletal Muscle Tissue:
* Describe the gross and microscopic anatomy of skeletal muscle tissue, including muscle fibers, myofibrils, sarcomeres, actin, and myosin.
* Explain the sliding filament theory of muscle contraction.

Skeletal Muscle Actions and Groups:
* Explain the concepts of origin, insertion, prime mover (agonist), antagonist, synergist, and fixator in muscle actions.
* Provide examples of major skeletal muscles and their actions in different body regions.

Clinical Relevance: Muscular Pathologies and Therapies:
* Briefly describe the causes, symptoms, and basic treatment approaches for common muscular disorders such as:
    * Muscle strains and sprains
    * Muscular dystrophy
    * Myasthenia gravis
* What are some common clinical therapies (e.g., rest, ice, compression, elevation, physical therapy)?
* What are some common pharmacological therapies used (e.g., pain relievers, muscle relaxants, immunosuppressants)?

**Module 7: The Nervous System: Basic Principles**

Organization of the Nervous System:
* Describe the basic divisions of the nervous system: central nervous system (CNS) and peripheral nervous system (PNS).
* Outline the subdivisions of the PNS (somatic and autonomic) and the autonomic nervous system (sympathetic and parasympathetic).

Neurons and Synapses:
* Describe the structure of a neuron and the function of its different parts (dendrites, cell body, axon, axon terminals).
* Explain the process of nerve impulse transmission (action potential).
* Describe the structure and function of a synapse, including the role of neurotransmitters.

Clinical Relevance: Neurological Disorders and Therapies:
* Briefly describe the causes, symptoms, and basic treatment approaches for common neurological disorders such as:
    * Stroke
    * Multiple sclerosis
    * Parkinson's disease
* What are some common clinical therapies (e.g., rehabilitation, physical therapy, occupational therapy)?
* What are some common pharmacological therapies used (e.g., thrombolytics, immunomodulators, dopamine agonists)?

**Module 8: The Endocrine System**

Hormones and Endocrine Glands:
* Define a hormone and explain the general mechanisms of hormone action.
* Classify hormones based on their chemical structure (e.g., steroid, peptide, amine).
* List the major endocrine glands in the body and identify the primary hormones they secrete.

Regulation of Hormone Secretion:
* Describe the different types of stimuli that control hormone release (humoral, neural, hormonal).
* Explain the concept of negative feedback in hormone regulation.

Clinical Relevance: Endocrine Disorders and Therapies:
* Briefly describe the causes, symptoms, and basic treatment approaches for common endocrine disorders such as:
    * Diabetes mellitus (Type 1 and Type 2)
    * Hypothyroidism and hyperthyroidism
    * Cushing's syndrome
* What are some common clinical therapies (e.g., lifestyle modifications, hormone replacement therapy)?
* What are some common pharmacological therapies used (e.g., insulin, oral hypoglycemic agents, thyroid hormones, glucocorticoid inhibitors)?

**Module 9: Integration and Clinical Applications**

Interactions Between Body Systems:
* Provide examples of how different organ systems in the body interact to maintain homeostasis (e.g., respiratory and cardiovascular systems, nervous and endocrine systems).

Homeostatic Imbalances and Disease:
* Explain the concept of homeostatic imbalance and how it can lead to disease.
* Discuss the importance of understanding anatomy and physiology in the diagnosis and treatment of various medical conditions.

Pharmacological Principles:
* Briefly discuss basic pharmacological principles relevant to A&P, such as pharmacokinetics (absorption, distribution, metabolism, excretion) and pharmacodynamics (mechanism of drug action).
* Explain how understanding normal physiology is crucial for understanding how drugs affect the body.
